Implementation Roadmap: Phased Strategy

The BIRD 2026–2035 translates strategy into three synchronized phases to ensure ecosystem maturity and global integration.

01

Foundation Building

2026 – 2028 | Est. Budget: ₱35–45B

Establishing foundational institutional elements and enabling conditions. This phase creates the environment without which subsequent phases cannot succeed.

  • Halal Certification: Operationalise the Bangsamoro Halal Board (BHB) with OIC/SMIIC-aligned certification processes.
  • Halal Park: Launch the Bangsamoro Halal Park development in Matanog as the flagship halal manufacturing ecozone.
  • Digital Registration: Implement digital business registration targeting 1-day processing via BEGMP.
  • Infrastructure: Achieve critical milestones in ZBIP and farm-to-market roads to remove binding constraints.
02

Acceleration

2029 – 2032 | Est. Budget: ₱50–65B

Scaling up successful Phase 1 initiatives. The reinforcing loops (R1 and R2) should be operating by this phase.

  • Full Halal Certification: Achieve full operationalisation with international OIC/SMIIC recognition and MRAs.
  • Halal Park Phase 1: Complete Phase 1 with anchor tenants in halal food, cosmetics, and pharmaceuticals.
  • Digital Infrastructure: Expand to 60% broadband penetration; implement e-government platform rollouts.
  • Islamic Banking: Establish Islamic banking operations; launch Takaful services targeting MSME sector.
03

Consolidation

2033 – 2035 | Est. Budget: ₱35–50B

Achieving the 2035 vision of a mature, self-sustaining halal hub. Transition from attraction to ecosystem management.

  • Full BHB Accreditation: Achieve full BHB international accreditation and mutual recognition with OIC member states.
  • Full Halal Park: Complete full development with comprehensive value chain facilities and BIMP-EAGA trade corridors.
  • Infrastructure Completion: Achieve 100% electrification and 85% broadband connectivity.
  • Regional Integration: Deepen BIMP-EAGA integration with established trade corridors and ₱50B intra-EAGA trade target.

Strategic Diagnostics: SWOT Analysis

Quantifying resilience, risk, and vulnerability across the Bangsamoro Economic and Investment Ecosystem (BEIE).

💪 Strengths

  • Halal Legitimacy & Cultural Credibility (RI=5.0)
  • Strategic Location in BIMP-EAGA (RI=4.0)
  • Strong Agriculture, Fisheries & Forestry Base
  • Domestic Halal Demand (5.69M consumers)

⚠️ Weaknesses

  • Weak Certification System (Risk=20.0)
  • Fragmented Policy Frameworks
  • Limited Infrastructure & Digital Connectivity
  • Skills Mismatch & Low Functional Literacy (59.3%)

🌟 Opportunities

  • Global Halal Market (USD 2.3T+)
  • ASEAN Integration & BIMP-EAGA Access
  • Islamic Finance Ecosystem Development
  • Green Economy & Carbon Markets (REDD+)

🔥 Threats

  • Competition from Established Halal Hubs
  • Standards Recognition Risks (OIC/SMIIC)
  • Climate Vulnerabilities & Environmental Shocks
  • Residual Security & Political Transition Risks
