The Spirit Behind the Survey

The C.A.R.E. Framework

4 Pillars · Khalifa-Aligned
C
Context-Specific
This survey was written for BARMM. Questions reflect real provincial realities -- Maguindanao's services economy, Lanao del Sur's agro-industrial corridor, Tawi-Tawi's seaweed farms -- so you're answering about the place you actually know.
A
Action-Oriented
Nothing here is asked just to fill space. Every question connects to a real decision -- where money goes, what gets built first, what policy gets prioritized. Your answers feed directly into those choices.
R
Real & Responsive
BARMM's economy doesn't move in a straight line. This survey is designed to capture that complexity through systems thinking archetypes, not force it into a simple, disconnected checklist.
E
Evidence-Based
Your answers are combined with verified government data, halal industry standards, academic research, and internationally recognized development benchmarks -- so your voice is weighed alongside solid evidence.

How Your Ratings Are Used

What Your 1-5 Ratings Actually Mean

4 Types of Questions
Questions About Strengths
  • You'll rate how strong something is, and how confident you are in that
  • Higher ratings tell us "this is a real strength we can build on"
  • Example: BARMM's halal identity and peace dividend momentum
  • Helps identify what BARMM should lean into, not just fix
Questions About Opportunities
  • You'll rate how promising an opportunity is, and how likely it is to happen
  • These help rank which opportunities are worth pursuing first
  • Example: The ASEAN halal market and BIMP-EAGA trade integration
  • Your view helps separate realistic bets from wishful thinking
Questions About Weaknesses
  • You'll rate how serious a gap or problem is, and how often it comes up
  • Higher ratings flag the issues holding BARMM back the most
  • Example: Infrastructure gaps and functional literacy rates
  • Your honesty here directly shapes what gets prioritized for fixing
Questions About Threats
  • You'll rate how damaging a threat could be, and how likely it is
  • Severe, likely threats get flagged for closer attention and planning
  • Example: Climate shocks, natural hazards, or market volatility
  • Helps the roadmap prepare for risks instead of being caught off guard
Your Privacy

Your Data, Protected (RA 10173 Compliant)

Data Privacy Act of 2012
Anonymized in Reporting
  • Your name and contact details are never published
  • Only aggregated, combined results appear in reports and dashboards
  • No individual response can be traced back to you publicly
  • You can answer honestly without fear of being personally identified
Handled Securely
  • Your response is transmitted and stored through secure, encrypted channels
  • Only authorized platform administrators can access raw submissions
  • The Bangsamoro Government (through BOI-MTIT BARMM) acts as the Personal Information Controller (PIC)
Your Rights Under RA 10173
  • Right to Access & Rectification: Request a copy of your responses or ask for corrections.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your responses prior to the aggregation phase.
  • Contact the BIRD team at bird-team@asilvainnovations.com to exercise these rights.
Full Privacy Policy
  • Participation is voluntary -- you may skip the survey or stop at any time
  • You'll be asked to explicitly consent before your response is submitted
  • Read the complete policy at: BIRD Privacy Policy

After You Hit Submit

What Happens to Your Answers Next

Your Voice, In Context
📊
Consensus Maps & Priority Matrices
Your ratings join hundreds of others to identify factors rated consistently high or low across all segments, creating clear priority matrices for planners.
🔍
Thematic Synthesis of Comments
If you write in your own words, those comments are read and grouped by theme. The BIRD development team reviews all qualitative input, not just the numbers.
🗺️
Provincial & Sectoral Breakdown
Results are segmented by province and organization type, ensuring priorities specific to Basilan, Tawi-Tawi, Maguindanao, or Lanao del Sur don't get lost in a regional average.
⚖️
Revised Systems Archetypes
Your validation of systems archetypes helps analysts identify recurring patterns that need structural fixes, rather than one-time symptomatic patches.
🎯
Strategy Confirmation
Your comparisons directly help confirm -- or challenge -- which overall strategy BARMM should lead with, as reviewed by government and development partner stakeholders.
📈
Live Public Dashboard
Aggregated (never individual) results feed a live results dashboard you can check anytime -- so you can see how your community's voice shows up in the bigger picture.

Key findings will also be presented and discussed at public validation workshops before the final BIRD 2026-2035 document is published.
